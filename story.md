## story_arc

1. **Wrong stop** — The narrator wakes in the Wrong Departure Lounge with no memory of arrival. Sera Venn explains that their home world has been filed under "pending archival deletion" and that they are now a registered transit passenger — though they never bought a ticket. The lounge's departure board lists only destinations nobody intends to reach.

2. **Gate seven-and-a-half** — A semi-existent platform appears only when no one is deliberately looking for it. Reaching it requires three forms, one polite lie, and one question good enough to embarrass the universe. Along the way: customs inspectors who speak in footnotes, semantic fog, and Unit 12 solving every problem in a way that makes things worse.

3. **The eternal passenger** — "The Eternal Passenger" is not a legend or a place but a person who has been waiting on the same bench at every station at once. The story resolves toward the Terminus Orchard or back to the lounge — whichever option is slightly less unreasonable.

## current_state

Situation:

You sit on a bench in the Wrong Departure Lounge that feels too familiar for a first visit. In your hand is a brass compass that points to where you were, not where you are going. On the table, a cup is empty and full at the same time.

Through the window, space stretches out in orderly rows — stars sorted by postal code.

The door opens without a sound. A woman steps in wearing a transit auditor's badge and an expression that suggests she has read the fine print on reality itself. She nods at you like an old colleague.

"Good — you're still here," she says. "Gate seven-and-a-half unmanifests in eight minutes. We should already be en route. Alternatively, we could finish our drinks, which is often the same thing."

She slides a small leather-bound book across the table. The cover reads: *Field Guide to Unscheduled Departures*. A page turns on its own and shows text you do not remember reading, though it feels like something you wrote in a dream.

Marginal note #0: Wrong departure lounges always sit at the point in the universe where maps and logic refuse to share a room.
